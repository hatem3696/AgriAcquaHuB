Rice is one of the most important cereal crops in the world.
It is a staple food for more than half of the world's population.
Rice is grown in flooded fields, known as paddy fields, or in dry fields.
There are more than 40,000 varieties of rice.
It is a good source of carbohydrates, vitamins, and minerals.
Rice can be cooked in various ways, such as boiled, steamed, fried, or baked.
It is used to make a variety of dishes, such as sushi, risotto, and biryani.
Rice bran oil is extracted from the outer layer of the rice grain and is used in cooking and as a health supplement.
The cultivation of rice dates back to more than 5,000 years ago in China.
Rice is the second most produced cereal crop after maize.
